E-Waste Intelligence System

India generates over 3.23 million metric tons of electronic waste annually — ranking 3rd globally. Yet only 22% is formally recycled, leaving toxic lead, mercury, and cadmium in landfills and unprotected waterways.

We built an AI-powered platform to predict, visualize, and guide policymakers in managing India's e-waste crisis before it becomes irreversible.

The Problem

Uncontrolled Growth: India's e-waste grew 151% over the past decade, driven by rising device penetration and rapid obsolescence cycles.
Informal Recycling Risks: 95% of e-waste is processed by informal workers without safety equipment, causing lead, mercury, and cadmium poisoning.
No Predictive Tools: Policymakers lack data-driven forecasting to proactively allocate resources and implement regulations ahead of waste peaks.
Urban Concentration: Top 5 cities account for 62% of all e-waste, yet lack targeted city-level policies and monitoring systems.
